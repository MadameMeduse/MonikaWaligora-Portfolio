People say that I'm talented. They are wrong though.

I believe talent is something you are born with... And I'm simply curious in many different areas. Ever since I was young, my parents encouraged me to answer questions that I myself asked and I often heard the phrase "well what do you think?".

They instilled in me the curiosity that germinated my entire childhood. I was curious how it is to transfer the three-dimensional world onto a flat sheet, which is why I enrolled in a drawing course at a near Art School.

I was curious to find out how it is to play on stage with several people, in one rhythm, connected with the joy of sound, so I learned to play the drums, which stole my heart and I play to this day.

I took part in workshops for leaders, because I wanted to learn how to communicate with others so that they listen, feel comfortable and want to cooperate. I was curious about how it is to live and study abroad, so I spend a year in Greece doing my master's studies.

I was employed at a marketing agency because it fascinated me to be able to direct my communication to a specific group of individuals in such a way that they would be willing to do things they would never have thought about before.

It was at this agency, during onboarding, that I first saw code. I was shown how to work with Google Analytics code, which allows tracking user traffic on the entire site by measuring time, clicks and other actions. This code had one line ...

I remember how improbable it seemed to me what I saw then and what it woke up in me ... curiosity I have never experienced before. I went to demo lessons at a programming school, where we worked on the completed code. I could not believe that through a few lines of text you can create such amazing things.

I started to study alone at home, I created my first website by replacing images and texts. When I saw the effect, I knew that in the end I found something that will never satisfy my curiosity - because programming science never ends.

Changing technologies, modern tools, the ability to improve my skills on every project. These are triggers that drive me and my brain to act. This curiosity has led me to coding, and a real passion for learning about the world. It's not a talent, I am working very hard to make my dream come true.
