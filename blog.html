Java Script workshops by WarsawJS

WarsawJS is a local community of JavaScript professionals and enthusiasts. They meet every month to talk and debate about their favorite programming language. This community is focused on sharing experience from different fields on which JS is used, from web browsers, to servers, dev tools, mobiles, smart tvs, micro controllers, databases, video games and so on.

I had the pleasure to attend Java Script workshops to broaden my programming knowledge in this language. There were many enthusiastic tutors such as @Bartosz Legięć, who managed my team, ready to help us with every problem we find in our project. We spend the whole day coding, sharing experiences and solving problems. It was a huge inspiration for me!

Hello Roman means hi Adam!

Roman or @Adam Romański is one of my favourite characters among programmers. And it's not because I had the opportunity to meet him personally and he turned out to be a very nice guy (it sounded like he was not, but he is), but because his YouTube channel @Hello Roman is a masterpiece.

I have never watched video tutorials before, because they are usually made by amateurs and are simply deadly boring. Adam / Roman does not say anything that is absolutely necessary to understand a given topic. Translates simply, bluntly and always with an example. At the beginning of my path of disguise, he gave me faith that I would succeed. He had previously worked as a copywriter in advertising agencies and was developing musically as a drummer, so we have almost the same background. And he succeeded. So why wouldn’t I?

In mid-May, I had the opportunity to participate in Hello Roman & Friends, the first Meet-up organised by Adam at the @Netguru headquarters in Poznań. It was an extraordinary experience that enriched me with another portion of knowledge about the IT world, and made me realize how a friendly relationship of an employee with a company can be, that does not just support the passion of its people, but actually helps them to realize it.

All those who are beginning their adventure with programming, as well as those already a bit more advanced, I encourage you to check out the Hello Roman youtube channel, and listen to one of his jokes at the beginning of each episode ;)
